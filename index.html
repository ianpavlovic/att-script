Phase 1 — Opening & Gatekeeper Navigation
Your goal in the first 15 seconds is to sound confident, identify the decision-maker, and earn permission to continue. Do NOT pitch the product yet.
Hello, my name is _______, an AT&T Solutions Provider calling on a recorded line. I'm trying to reach the person who handles internet services for the business — would that be you, or could you point me to the right person?
💡 GATEKEEPER STRATEGY
If they say the DM is unavailable: "No problem — I'm reaching out because AT&T has pre-qualified your business location for a new 5G internet backup service. Could I get the best time and direct number to reach [DM name]?"
If you reach the DM directly, confirm their role: "Are you the person who manages internet and connectivity decisions for the business?" Wait for YES before proceeding.
Phase 2 — Hook & Engagement
This is the most critical moment. You have ~20 seconds to earn their attention. Lead with a problem they recognize, not a product name.
Great — the reason for my call is that your business at [ADDRESS] has been pre-qualified for AT&T's Fixed Wireless Internet service.
Quick question — has your business ever experienced an internet outage that disrupted operations or cost you revenue?
💡 WHY THIS WORKS
This question does two things: (1) It gets the prospect talking, which builds engagement. (2) It surfaces pain you can solve. If they say YES: "Exactly — that's precisely why businesses like yours are adding this service."
If they say NO or seem dismissive: "That's great to hear. Most businesses don't think about it until it happens — and the average cost of just one hour of downtime for a small business is over $10,000. This service is designed to make sure that never becomes your reality."
Phase 3 — Value Proposition
Deliver this section conversationally, not as a script read. Hit the key points but adapt to the prospect's responses.
Even if you have fiber, cable, or a shared connection today, outages happen — and when they do, every system that depends on the internet goes down with it: your point-of-sale, your phones, your email, your customer-facing tools.
AT&T Fixed Wireless Internet gives your business a dedicated 5G wireless backup connection with average download speeds between 300 and 474 Mbps. It works independently from your wired connection, so if one goes down, the other keeps you running.
And here's the part that makes this a no-brainer — let me walk you through the pricing.
Phase 4 — Pricing & Offer Presentation
Stack the value. Present the full price first, then reveal discounts one at a time to create a "price drop" effect.
AT&T Fixed Wireless Premium normally runs $105 per month and includes 250GB of priority data for your critical business applications.
But right now, AT&T has reduced the price to as low as $30 per month when you enroll in paperless billing and autopay with existing AT&T wireless service.
On top of that, the 5G wireless router — which normally costs $275 — is available right now for just $1 on a 3-year term agreement. And AT&T is waiving all shipping fees.
So for roughly $4 a day, your business gets a fully independent backup internet connection backed by AT&T's 5G network and the AT&T Business Guarantee.
You will receive a dedicated bill for AT&T Fixed Wireless Internet, so this will be billed separately from any other AT&T services you may have.
💡 OBJECTION — "That sounds expensive"
Reframe: "I understand — let me put it this way. One hour of downtime can cost a business thousands in lost revenue and productivity. This service costs roughly $4 a day. It's not an expense — it's insurance for your business operations."
Phase 5 — Trial Close & Transition
Based on what I've shared, does it make sense to get this set up as a backup for your business?
Wait for a response. If YES → proceed to Phase 6. If HESITANT → handle objection. If NO → ask what their concern is and address it.
💡 OBJECTION — "I need to think about it"
"Absolutely, I respect that. Just so you know, the $1 router promotion and waived shipping are current limited-time offers. What specific concern would you want to think through? Maybe I can help clarify right now."
💡 OBJECTION — "We already have a backup"
"That's smart — what are you using currently? [Listen] One advantage of AT&T Fixed Wireless is that it runs on a completely separate 5G network, so it's not dependent on the same infrastructure as your primary or existing backup."
Phase 6 — Qualification & Data Collection ✏️
Perfect. To get the AT&T agreement sent over to you today, I just need to confirm a few quick details about your business.
Ask questions in this order: low-friction items first, sensitive items (Tax ID) last. Keep momentum — use a conversational cadence, not an interrogation.
💡 TAX ID OBJECTION HANDLER
If they hesitate: "I completely understand the sensitivity. AT&T requires this solely to verify existing wireless accounts and to process the new service agreement. It's a standard business verification step — similar to what you'd provide for any new business utility."

This section is required for regulatory compliance. Read it clearly and at a moderate pace. Do not rush through it.

You can choose to retain premium service and enroll in paperless billing and autopay for additional discounts, or switch to standard service for as low as $30 per month with applicable discounts.
Billing begins upon device shipment and renews monthly. You may cancel anytime by calling AT&T. This is a 3-year term of service. As a reminder, you will receive a dedicated bill for AT&T Fixed Wireless Internet, separate from any other AT&T services.
The agreement we email covers standard AT&T wireless policy and does not contain any pricing.
Will you have any trouble accepting the agreement today?
Wait for confirmation. If they have concerns, address them. Do not proceed until you have verbal consent.
